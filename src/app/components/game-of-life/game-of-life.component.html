Guide

How to use it

  • Edit the field while paused by clicking or dragging across cells.
  • Start runs the simulation continuously using the current board state.
  • Next step advances one generation without entering autoplay.
  • Randomize is useful for finding fast-moving or self-organizing patterns.
  • Pick a pattern type and shape, then click the board to stamp it.
Mechanics

What's actually happening

Every cell is either alive or dead, and each generation every cell's fate is decided purely by how many of its 8 neighbors are alive right now — no memory, no randomness, just four rules:

  • Underpopulation — a live cell with fewer than 2 live neighbors dies.
  • Survival — a live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbors stays alive.
  • Overpopulation — a live cell with more than 3 live neighbors dies.
  • Reproduction — a dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors becomes alive.

All of these rules are applied to the whole board at once to produce the next generation, which is what makes shapes like gliders appear to move. The board also wraps at its edges, so anything that drifts off one side re-enters on the opposite side instead of disappearing.
